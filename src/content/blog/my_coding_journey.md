## What is coding to begin with?
Coding was something mysterious to me. Back in the day, I wasn't a techsavy person to begin with. I remember my cousing was always talking about computers and hacking but I didn't understand a thing. I did take TIC courses back in middle school and high school, but they didn't cover any coding topics, it was just your basic MS Word or Excel usage classes.

## You are not going to be a doctor

When I was in 7th grade (2017), I wanted to become a doctor. To this date I don't remember what was appealing about the career but I wanted to be either a Neurosurgeon or a Traumatologist. Lets say it didn't go that way. One day I had to get my blood drawn and I ended up fainting. This happened several times in the span of three years. By the time I was in 9th grade, I realized that being a doctor wasn't a reallistic career for me. 
So I started to search for other career options. I started searching for something STEM-related during the school year, but I didn't find anything appealing. One day I was browsing my YouTube feed, when I decided to search for a coding tutorial. I found [Codecademy's Python Tutorial for Begginers](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfscVS0vtbw&t=99s) and I decided to start learning the easiest programming language. At the time, my father had lent me a Toshiba laptop. It was kinda old, but it did the trick. I installed VS Code and Python and started following the tutorial. At first it was challenging, but I liked the challenge. When I finished the tutorial, I realized there was a whole other world in tech. So that's when I decided to study computer engineering.

## The Lockdown
At the beginning of my 10th grade year, the COVID-19 pandemic started. The first part of that year was stressful for me, as the schoolwork was getting to insane levels and I was undergoing a running injury. Then we went into lockdown and that allowed me to code on my new free time. I did some CodeWars problems and decided to learn C++ for fun.
I started to watch a lot of content about software development and the tech industry. This really inspired me to continue coding and learning new things. After the lockdown, I decided to major in Computer Engineering at college. 

## The Lesson
I learnt that most of the time, things don't go as we desire. Life has a weird way of working, sometimes we might think we are going in the opposite direction of what we want. But I have learnt that things don't go the way we want, they go the way we need, whether we acknowledge that or not. So if you are interested in some beginner coding resources, I will leave them down. :)

### Coding resources to begin your journey
- [Codecademy's Python Tutorial for Begginers](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfscVS0vtbw&t=99s) - The fist coding tutorial I watched. 
- [Geeks For Geeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org) - A website that I used as a resource for better understanding concepts.
- [CodeWars](https://www.codewars.com) - Where I learned to solve coding problems in different programming languages.
- [edX](https://www.edx.org) - They offer great courses and get certificates.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare](https://ocw.mit.edu) - A lot of MIT courses for free.
